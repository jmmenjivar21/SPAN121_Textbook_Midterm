Objetivos del Repaso

By the end of Semana 5 you will be able to:

Conjugate ser, llamarse, and regular -ar/-er/-ir verbs in the present tense
Use the correct definite article (el / la / los / las) with classroom vocabulary
Conjugate the irregular verbs ir, tener, and hacer fluently
Apply adjective agreement (gender + number) consistently in writing and speech
Use gustar with correct singular/plural and indirect-object pronouns
Choose between este / ese / aquel based on distance and noun agreement
Write numbers 1–50 in Spanish words
Read a short Spanish paragraph and answer comprehension questions
Write a 6–8 sentence composition using all chapter structures

Mi profesora favorita se llama la Dra. Ramírez. Ella es mexicana, divertida y muy carismática. En su clase escuchamos música en español, leemos cuentos cortos y escribimos composiciones. Aprendemos rápido porque ella es una profesora excelente.

Después de las clases, Lucía y yo escuchamos música. A mí me gusta Shakira porque es una cantante colombiana muy talentosa. A Lucía le gustan más los músicos cubanos y argentinos. 💡 Top Tips · Consejos del día

1. Manage time — don't spend more than the listed minutes on each section. Move on and come back.

2. Read every prompt twice. Many errors come from misreading "él" as "tú" or missing a plural marker.

3. Use the 7-item checklist in Ejercicio L on your writing answer before submitting.

4. If you don't know a word in writing, paraphrase. Don't leave the question blank.

5. Accents matter — tú (you) ≠ tu (your), sí (yes) ≠ si (if).

Errores Comunes · Common Errors to Avoid

The mistakes graders see most often

❌ Common error✓ CorrectWhy
una actriz talentoso una actriz talentosa adjective must agree with feminine noun
me gusta los libros me gustan los libros "libros" is plural → use gustan
yo voy (present)…ayer match tense to time marker don't mix present with past time markers
yo soy 19 años yo tengo 19 años use tener for age, not ser
¿Que es tu nombre? ¿Cuál es tu nombre? cuál for selection, qué for definition
la problema el problema words ending in -ma from Greek are usually masculine
